Overview

LPADCCA converts 12-bit LPADC samples (3.072 MHz) to PCM audio at 8, 16, or 32 kHz. The primary design goal is sub-10 μW total power at the 12nm 0.668 V supply.

Signal chain: ADC capture → CIC decimator (sinc³) → 4× HB19 halfband stages → LPF FIR → DGA (programmable gain) → HPF (DC block) → 16-deep output FIFO → APB3 slave (Cortex-M55).

Power target of < 10 μW achieved — 6.21 μW, 38% margin.

Leakage dominates (58%) due to always-on filter delay-line registers at 0.668 V. NB mode is the lowest-power operating point (most stages clock-gated). WB and SWB will be modestly higher as more clock domains remain active. Worst-case 100% toggle activity estimate: 14.6 μW. 17 integrated clock-gating cells are in place.

Frequency Response

SWB passband is within ~2 dB. NB and WB show up to ~3 dB droop at band edges due to HB/LPF passband ripple and HPF interaction (open issue #18). Fix: equiripple LPF coefficient redesign — no RTL or power changes required.
